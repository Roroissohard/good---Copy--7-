The Exotic Axelotl

An Ambystoma better known as an axelotl is a neotenic salamander. They're home is in mexico by freshwater lakes and ponds. Food consists of small fish, worms and other small aquatic animals. These salamanders are a independant spiecies not traveling or hunting in packs.

Endagerment

They are very fragile so they live in captivity for 10 - 15 years. The reason also being Axelotls are critically endangered creatures and experience a lot of habitat loss and pollution.

Fun Facts

Some fun facts include their display of colors like albino, gold, pink and an even more wide range of colors. They are small ranging from 6 to 18 inches and they can even regrow their limbs, heart and brain. Axelotl's are very adorable creatures and it would be sad to see another exotic species go extinct
